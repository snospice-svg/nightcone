Fourteen nights, 8 mg, taken on a wake-back-to-bed schedule about 4.5 hours into sleep. Three lucid dreams — the strongest lucidity signal in the matrix to date.

Galantamine slows the breakdown of acetylcholine, the neurotransmitter most tied to REM and dream vividness, and the WBTB timing places it right as REM density climbs. Recall ran high but fragmented; the back half of the night felt lighter, consistent with the mechanism.

The honest framing is that this is real and unreliable at the same time. Lucidity showed up on a minority of nights, never on command, and tolerance builds fast enough that nightly dosing is the wrong move. Respect the effect; keep testing the conditions.
