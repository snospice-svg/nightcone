The newest entry isolates Acacia xanthophloea — the African fever tree — from the ubulawu blends it normally appears in, and tests it on its own.

The result is the verdict this thread keeps reaching. Pulled out of the froth-prepared mixture it belongs to, the single plant carries little of the blend's reputation: recall near baseline, no lucidity, no clear vividness signal. It is widely sold as a dream herb; its mechanism is basically unknown.

That's the whole point of testing components individually. A blend that "works" tells you nothing about which ingredient matters. Isolating the variable is slower and less satisfying, and it's the only way to learn anything you can trust.
