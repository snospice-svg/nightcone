Galantamine's cousin: the same enzyme target (acetylcholinesterase) at roughly double the half-life. The pharmacological rationale is sound — if galantamine works on dreams through cholinergic potentiation, huperzine should too.

In practice it underdelivered. Recall nudged up modestly, lucidity barely registered, and the long half-life (~10–14 hours) meant the effect bled into the following night, muddying the protocol. It also produced a headache on waking on several mornings. Paired with blue lotus on a few nights, there was no additive effect.

A plausible hypothesis still waiting on real data. The dream-specific evidence remains anecdotal — there's no controlled trial — and the long clearance makes it harder to run cleanly than galantamine.
