Acacia xanthophloea — the fever tree — appears as one component in certain Southern African ubulawu dream blends. Nightcone tests it in isolation precisely because a blend can't tell you which plant, if any, is doing the work.

Isolated, it produced little: recall hovered at or just below baseline, no lucidity, no clear vividness signal. The mechanism is basically unknown — there's no pharmacological characterisation tying it to sleep or dreaming, only its role inside a traditional mixture.

This is the recurring verdict of the ubulawu thread: pulled out of the froth-prepared blend it belongs to, a single component rarely carries the reputation of the whole. Presented with cultural context, not as marketing mysticism, and with the mechanism stated plainly as unknown.
