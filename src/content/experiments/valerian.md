One of the most widely used herbal sleep aids on the planet, in use since at least the second century. The active compounds — valerenic acid and the valepotriates — appear to modulate GABA-A receptors, the same broad system benzodiazepines act on, at much lower intensity.

The interesting hypothesis is REM rebound: if valerian mildly suppresses early-night REM, dreaming could concentrate into the later cycles when dreams are longest and most memorable. It's plausible and, as far as the literature goes, untested — no study has paired valerian with EEG specifically to measure REM redistribution.

In testing, no dream benefit appeared: recall sat at or below baseline, and some logs elsewhere report valerian *suppressing* recall rather than enhancing it. Deeper initial sleep, maybe; richer dreams, no. Whether it shifts dream architecture or just deepens sleep stays an open question — but on this protocol it did nothing for dreaming.
