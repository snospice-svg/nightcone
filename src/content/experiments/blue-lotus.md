Nymphaea caerulea — the blue lotus the Egyptians depicted everywhere, widely sold today as an "ancient dream plant." Nine nights on the compound, three of them stacked with huperzine A to probe for an additive effect.

The headline is restraint. Recall sat around baseline most nights, vividness nudged up occasionally, narrative coherence didn't move, and there was no lucidity and no hypnagogic imagery at any point. Dreams that did register had a calmer tone — a soft effect, if it was an effect at all.

Timing and dose didn't clearly matter: pre-bed and 3am dosing landed in the same range, and a later seven-night run at roughly twice the typical dose didn't change the verdict. Whatever the Egyptians were doing with this flower, it was not this.
