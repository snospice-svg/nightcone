The most evidenced compound in the lucid-dreaming space, and the one that produced Nightcone's clearest lucidity signal: three lucid dreams across the fourteen-night run.

Galantamine is an acetylcholinesterase inhibitor — it slows the breakdown of acetylcholine, the neurotransmitter most directly tied to REM sleep and dream vividness. Taken on a wake-back-to-bed schedule roughly 4.5 hours into sleep, it lands right as REM density climbs.

Recall was high but fragmented; sleep felt lighter in the back half of the night, consistent with the mechanism. The effect is real enough to respect and unreliable enough to keep testing — lucidity appeared on a minority of nights, not on demand. Worth noting: tolerance builds quickly, which is why the lucid-dreaming community spaces galantamine several nights apart rather than dosing nightly.
