The rare oneirogen with a clean trial behind it. Aspy et al. (2018) ran a double-blind, placebo-controlled study with around 100 participants and found that high-dose B6 before sleep increased dream recall — without changing vividness, bizarreness, emotional intensity, or colour.

Testing here reproduced the shape of that result: recall went up, and the rest stayed flat. B6 appears to act specifically on *remembering* the dream, not on the dream itself — a recall enhancer, not a dream modifier.

That distinction matters for the whole project. A compound can move the needle on one axis and leave the others untouched, and lumping "dream effects" together hides that. B6 is the cleanest example: suggestive, backed by a single good study, and narrowly scoped to recall.
