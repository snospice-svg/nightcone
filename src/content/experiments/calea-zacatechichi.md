The strongest of the traditional dream herbs tested so far. Calea zacatechichi — the "dream herb" of the Chontal people of Oaxaca — produced genuinely more vivid and more bizarre dream narratives, with recall noticeably above baseline on most nights.

The cost is the taste: intensely, almost punishingly bitter, and at higher prep concentrations it brought mild nausea on the way to sleep. Dream content skewed strange — disjointed scenes, unusual juxtapositions — more than it skewed lucid, though lucidity did surface a couple of times.

Mechanism is unstudied. The plant contains sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids, but no controlled work ties any of them to dream effects. Filed as: probably does something, mechanism unknown, worth a longer run.
