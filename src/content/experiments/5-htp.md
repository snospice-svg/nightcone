5-HTP is a direct precursor to serotonin, and the folklore is that it intensifies dreams — usually framed as a second-half-of-the-night REM-rebound effect, sometimes paired with claims about vivid or disturbing dreams on withdrawal.

On this protocol, at 100 mg, none of that reproduced. Recall sat at baseline, vividness didn't move, and there were no notable anomalies across the run. Whatever effect others report either needs a different dose, different timing, or simply didn't survive a single-subject test against a noisy baseline.

Logged as null on these conditions — not a claim that 5-HTP does nothing under all conditions, just that it did nothing measurable here.
