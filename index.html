What is Nightcone Labs?

Nightcone Labs investigates compounds, plants, and protocols that may alter dream recall, dream vividness, dream intensity, and the probability of lucid dreaming. The project is grounded in skeptical, structured experimentation rather than recycled folklore.

The aim is to study dreaming as a system that may be influenced, measured, and gradually better understood through disciplined observation and repeated trials.

Core areas of focus

Oneirogen testing

Structured trials involving dream-active botanicals, extracts, and compounds associated with dream modulation.

Lucid dreaming

Testing whether specific compounds or routines change the likelihood, clarity, or stability of lucid dreams.

Dream measurement

Developing useful ways to score dream recall, vividness, coherence, emotional intensity, and lucidity.

Protocol development

Moving from isolated experiences toward methods that can be repeated, compared, challenged, and improved.

Why this exists

The world of dream herbs and oneirogens is full of vague claims, inconsistent reports, and weak standardization. Nightcone Labs exists to bring more rigor to that territory.

The central question is whether dream states can be influenced in ways that are not just dramatic, but reliable, measurable, and worth replicating.

Nightcone Labs is an attempt to turn dream-state exploration into a more disciplined experimental practice.

Topics explored

Oneirogens. Lucid dreaming. Dream herbs. Dream recall. Dream vividness. Dream-state experimentation. Ethnobotanical dream plants. Self-trials. Protocol design. Null results. Repeated observation.

This includes attention to plants and compounds commonly discussed in dream research circles, including mugwort, Silene capensis, and Synaptolepis kirkii.
